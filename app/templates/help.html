How to navigate through ShowMe.news

ShowMe.news is a dynamic news clustering system that allows users to explore news articles based on their interests. By entering specific keywords, users can quickly retrieve relevant news articles, group them into meaningful clusters, and explore related topics in an organized manner. This approach provides a powerful tool for organizing and analyzing large amounts of news data in real time, enabling users to identify trends, explore topics of interest, and gain a better understanding of complex news stories and their relationships.

ShowMe.news enhances the user experience by providing a visual and interactive way to explore news content, making it easier to discover and engage with relevant information. Whether you are looking for the latest news on a specific topic or want to explore trending stories, ShowMe.news offers a dynamic and user-friendly platform for exploring news articles based on your interests.

How to use ShowMe.news?

To use ShowMe.news, simply enter one or more keywords in the search bar and click "Search". The system will retrieve relevant news articles based on your keywords and display them in an organized manner. You can explore the news clusters by clicking on the nodes in the visualization, which represent different topics or themes. By interacting with the visualization, you can navigate through the news clusters, view related articles, and gain insights into the news content. You can zoom in or zoom out of the visualization to explore the news clusters in more detail.

ShowMe.news provides a user-friendly interface that allows you to explore news articles in a dynamic and interactive way, making it easy to discover and engage with relevant information.

Eye-Tracking and Cluster Interaction

ShowMe.news incorporates an innovative eye-tracking feature that enhances the user interaction experience. By leveraging eye-tracking technology, users can hover over a cluster in the visualization, and the system automatically selects the cluster and displays relevant documents associated with it.

When you hover your mouse (or gaze, in the case of eye-tracking systems) over a cluster node, a context menu or relevant options such as "Show Documents" will appear. You can use these options to explore the articles related to that cluster without needing to click, allowing for seamless navigation and exploration.

This feature is particularly useful for users who want to quickly browse through topics and identify key clusters of interest without any additional clicks, making the interface even more intuitive and responsive.

Calibration Step: Setting Up Eye-Tracking

To make the most of the eye-tracking feature, it's essential to calibrate the system before use. The calibration process ensures accuracy in detecting your gaze and associating it with clusters in the visualization. Follow these steps to calibrate:

  • When you first access the system, a calibration popup will appear. Click "Start" to begin.
  • Interact with the nine calibration points displayed on the screen. Click on each point five times until it turns yellow.
  • Once all points are calibrated, stare at the center of the screen for five seconds to calculate accuracy.
  • You can recalibrate at any time by selecting the "Recalibrate" option in the navigation menu.
